Rice Blast Disease

Rice blast is a fungal disease affecting rice crops.

Symptoms include spindle-shaped lesions on rice leaves. The lesions may
have gray or white centers with brown or reddish borders.

The disease may affect leaves, nodes, collars, and panicles.

Management practices include using resistant rice varieties, maintaining
balanced fertilizer application, avoiding excessive nitrogen application,
and following appropriate field sanitation practices.

Farmers should follow locally approved agricultural recommendations when
selecting fungicides or other plant protection products.
